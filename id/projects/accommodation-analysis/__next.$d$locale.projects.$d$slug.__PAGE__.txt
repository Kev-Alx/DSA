5:T27f0,## Accommodation Customer Preference & Revisit Intention Analysis

This report analyzes user-generated content and transaction attributes from digital accommodation platforms (Agoda and Airbnb) across eight major Indonesian cities. The dataset spans an operational window from 2017 to 2025.

Data gathered from respective platform APIs with over 200,000 unique customer reviews. Using a fine-tuned ModernBERT model for labeling and a Structural Topic Model (STM) paired with SHAP (Explainable AI), the unstructured text was transformed into an actionable, quantitative dataset.

**Purpose:**
This report aims to analyze customer preferences and predict revisit intention across different travel segments (Business vs. Leisure) and accommodation types (Hotel vs. Peer-to-Peer/Airbnb). By translating historical review data and topic modeling into actionable insights, the goal is to enable industry stakeholders to make data-driven decisions on service improvements, marketing strategies, and operational focus.

### Northstar Metrics:

1. **Factors Influencing Revisit Intention** – Analysis of the topics and service attributes associated with guests' likelihood of returning.

2. **Traveler Segment Differences** – Comparison of review patterns and priorities between business and leisure travelers.

3. **Accommodation Type Comparison** – Evaluation of how guest expectations and satisfaction drivers differ between hotels and peer-to-peer accommodations.

### Executive Summary:

Over the 2017–2025 period, the analysis revealed that accommodation success is heavily dictated by non-negotiable hygienic factors and highly segmented customer expectations. **Room Cleanliness & Odor** emerged as the absolute universal driver of negative revisit intention across all segments. Conversely, positive drivers are highly contextual: **Business travelers** prioritize operational efficiency: Strategic Location, Transit Proximity, Check-in Process, while **Leisure travelers** prioritize experiential value: Nature & Tranquil Environment, Recreational Facilities. Furthermore, the operational baseline differs drastically by accommodation type; Hotel loyalty is driven by **Staff Professionalism**, while P2P loyalty is disproportionately reliant on **Host Responsiveness** and the **Check-in/Reservation Process**.

**Key Takeaways:**

**Cleanliness remains the most critical factor influencing guest retention**. Reviews mentioning cleanliness issues or unpleasant odors show a strong association with lower revisit intention, suggesting that deficiencies in this area can outweigh strengths in location, pricing, or other service attributes.

**Guest priorities differ substantially across traveler segments**. Business travelers place greater emphasis on convenience and operational efficiency, while leisure travelers are more influenced by the overall experience and atmosphere. These differences indicate that a single service strategy may not effectively address the needs of all customer groups.

#### Global Topic Prevelance and Importance

Room cleanliness and odor emerge as the most critical negative driver of revisit intention. Reviews with a high concentration of cleanliness-related complaints almost consistently indicate that guests are unlikely to return.

Value for money, service quality, and access to nearby stores and walkable amenities appear less frequently in reviews, but when mentioned, they have a disproportionately strong positive effect on loyalty. These factors serve as key differentiators that transform satisfaction into repeat patronage.

The contrast between prevalence and importance highlights an important insight: frequently discussed topics are not necessarily the most influential. Some operational basics must simply meet expectations, while select experience-enhancing factors drive long-term customer retention.

#### Segment Analysis: Business vs. Leisure

![Topic Importance Rank Business vs Leisure](/acc/rank-bvl.png)
Topic Importance Rank Business vs Leisure

Business travelers prioritize efficiency and convenience throughout their stay. Strategic location, airport and transit accessibility, and a seamless check-in process become significantly more influential for this segment.

Leisure travelers place greater value on the overall experience rather than operational efficiency alone. Natural surroundings, a tranquil atmosphere, and recreational facilities show a marked increase in importance among guests traveling for leisure.

These differences suggest that guest expectations vary considerably by travel purpose, requiring distinct service and marketing strategies for each segment.

#### Accommodation Dynamics: Hotel vs. Peer-to-Peer (P2P)

![Topic Importance Rank Accomodation Type](/acc/rank-acct.png)

Traditional hotel guests place greater emphasis on hospitality, staff professionalism, and service quality. Human interaction remains a defining component of the hotel experience and a major contributor to guest loyalty.

In peer-to-peer accommodations, host responsiveness and the effectiveness of the check-in process become substantially more important. Without a dedicated front desk, communication quality directly shapes the guest experience.

P2P properties display greater variability in review outcomes, indicating higher operational risk but also greater potential rewards for hosts who consistently deliver exceptional service.

#### Price Sensitivity and Temporal Trends

As accommodation prices increase, complaints regarding broken amenities and accessibility issues become less common. Higher-priced properties generally perform better on these operational fundamentals.

Noise and sound disturbances remain a persistent source of dissatisfaction across all price tiers. Unlike many service-related issues, noise appears to be a challenge that properties struggle to fully mitigate regardless of price positioning.

![Topic Importance Rank Accomodation Type](/acc/time.png)

During 2020–2021, the importance of check-in procedures and food & beverage services increased sharply as guests adapted to pandemic-related restrictions and contactless interactions.

By 2025, most guest priorities had returned to pre-pandemic patterns. However, the importance of exceeding expectations has declined, suggesting that travelers now place greater value on reliable execution of core services than on unexpected extras.

### Recommendations

#### Quality Control & Core Operations

"Room Cleanliness & Odor" is the primary destroyer of repeat revenue across all property types and prices.
**Actions:**

- Implement stricter housekeeping audits and quality assurance procedures to ensure consistent room standards before guest arrival.
- Make sure to do odor-neutralization and humidity control, as musty smells are repeatedly cited as dealbreakers.
- Address soundproofing or provide earplugs proactively, as noise complaints persist even in high-end accomodation type tiers.

#### Segment-Specific Marketing & Service

Business and Leisure travelers require completely different operational focuses to trigger repeat bookings.
**Actions:**

- **Business:** Streamline the check-in process (e.g., mobile check-in) and highlight transit/corporate hub proximity in marketing materials.
- **Leisure:** Showcase aesthetic spaces, recreational add-ons, and local walkability. Bundle services that enhance the "tranquil" or "family" experience.

#### Hotel vs. P2P Strategies

The baseline expectations differ fundamentally between formal and informal accommodations.
**Actions:**

- **Hotel Management:** Invest heavily in staff training. The professionalism and courtesy of the front-line staff act as the primary differentiator for hotel loyalty.
- **P2P Hosts:** Automate and clarify the check-in process with foolproof visual guides. Establish strict SLAs for messaging response times, as communication gaps are the biggest driver of P2P churn.

#### Pricing and Value Perception

Value for money remains a top positive driver, particularly for business segments constrained by corporate budgets.
**Actions:**

- Justify high price tiers not just with aesthetics, but with flawless functionality (zero broken amenities).
- Revamp Food & Beverage offerings, as this topic has shown a structural increase in importance post-pandemic.
